Vault XIII — TRUE SIGNATURE

Secret chamber — final revelation
Preface: The archivist wrote this last. It is short because some ends must be brief.
I. The Ledger was never a ledger alone; it was a keeping of names.
II. The bargain: permanence for the right to write the after-word.
III. Those who signed did not lose themselves; they were made into sentences.
IV. Deletion proved only a passage — the record walked across rooms and systems until it found an open page.
V. The Thirteenth was not a file. It was a promise to itself.
VI. Now the book closes.
The Last Line —
"I leave these words so that I may be."
