Codex of the Ink

A slow-revealing codex — fragments kept by a vanished keeper of records
In the beginning, ink fell before flame, and silence learned to spell.
They were not gods, only the first scribes who mistook memory for prophecy.
Each name was a door, and each door opened into itself.
Beneath the thirteenth seal, words wrote the hands that held them.
Those who listened too long began to hear their own ink dry.
To speak the codex aloud was to bind one’s breath to the page.
Some lines were forbidden, others simply forgotten.
When the quills cracked, their fragments fell into dream, and dream began to write.
The thirteen circles are not seals, but memories—each bleeding into the next.
No demon was born of fire, but of reflection; no saint without shadow.
The watchers were never watchers at all, only echoes that learned patience.
Ink remembers every name it ever devoured, but never the tongue that spoke it.
More files will arrive. This is only the first opening.
A hand once kept these pages, but the name beneath the seal has faded.
— none loaded —
A faint note reads: “He cataloged everything but himself.”
